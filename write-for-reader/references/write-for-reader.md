# Write for reader

* Less is more
* Make Reading Easy
* Design for Easy Navigation
* Use Enough Formatting but No More
* Tell readers why they should care
* Make responding easy

## Less is more
* Use fewer words
* Include fewer ideas
* Make fewer requests

Example:
```
<NG>
I am a professor at Harvard studying the opinions, decision-making, goals, and
expectations of school board members. As a school board member, you have an
important and difficult job. You and your fellow school board members are making
critical decisions right now that will profoundly impact the lives of students,
teachers, and families in your schools and communities. I know you are busy with
many urgent and important decisions as your schools reopen. School district
leaders like you are balancing many competing interests. Your participation will
be very helpful to the research I am conducting. I would like to learn from you
how school district leaders are thinking about the challenges facing schools
right now. Would you please complete this brief survey? The link is here:
http://surveylink.example.com.

Dr. Rogers, Professor of Public Policy

<OK>
I am a professor at Harvard studying school board members. Your participation
will be very helpful to the research I am conducting. Would you please complete
this brief survey? The link is here: http://surveylink.example.com.

Dr. Rogers, Professor of Public Policy
```

## Make Reading Easy
* Use short and common words
* Write straightforward sentences
* Write shorter sentences

Example:
```
<NG>
We need to utilize our collaborative framework to facilitate the implementation
of the new initiative. This endeavor will necessitate coordination among all
stakeholders, and we should commence the process immediately in order to achieve
optimal results.

<OK>
We need to use our team plan to start the new project. This will require working
with all partners. We should begin now to get the best results.
```

## Design for Easy Navigation
* Make key information immediately visible
* Separate distinct ideas
* Place related ideas together
* Order ideas by priority
* Include headings
* Consider using visuals

```
<NG>
Subject: Project Update

Hi team, I wanted to update you on the Q4 marketing campaign. We've completed
the initial research phase and the results are very promising. Our target
audience responded well to the new messaging. The budget has been approved
by finance and we're ready to move forward. We need everyone to review the
creative materials by Friday. The timeline has been adjusted and the launch
date is now December 15th instead of December 1st. Please update your calendars.
Also, we're lo<OK>ing for volunteers to help with the event setup. Let me know
if you're interested. Thanks for all your hard work on this project.

<OK>
Subject: Q4 Marketing Campaign Update

**Key Points:**
- Launch date moved to December 15th
- Action needed: Review materials by Friday

**Research Results**
Our target audience responded well to the new messaging.

**Budget Status**
Finance approved the full budget.

**Next Steps**
1. Review creative materials by Friday
2. Update calendars with new December 15th launch
3. Volunteer for event setup (optional)

Questions? Reply to this email.
```

## Use Enough Formatting but No More
* Match formatting to readers’ expectations
* Highlight, bold, or underline the most important ideas
* Limit your formatting

```
<NG-Too many emphasize>
Subject: **URGENT**: Q4 Sales Report

Hi Team,

I need to **share** some **IMPORTANT** updates about our **Q4 performance**.

**First**, our **revenue** increased by **15%** compared to **last quarter**.
This is **excellent news** and shows that our **strategy** is **working**.

**Second**, we need to **focus** on the **following priorities**:
- **Improve** customer **retention**
- **Expand** our **marketing** efforts
- **Enhance** product **quality**

Please **review** this **carefully** and let me know if you have **ANY questions**.

**Thanks**,
**Sarah**

<OK>
Subject: Q4 Sales Report - Key Updates

Hi Team,

Q4 Performance Update:

**Revenue increased 15%** compared to last quarter. This shows our strategy
is working.

Our priorities for next quarter:
- Improve customer retention
- Expand marketing efforts
- Enhance product quality

Please review and let me know if you have questions.

Thanks,
Sarah
```

## Tell readers why they should care
* Emphasize what readers value (“So what?”)
* Emphasize which readers should care (“Why me?”)

Example:
```
<NG>
Subject: System Maintenance Notification

Dear Users,

Our IT department will be performing scheduled system maintenance next week.
This is part of our regular infrastructure upgrade program that we conduct
annually to ensure optimal performance and security. The maintenance window
has been carefully selected based on our analysis of historical usage patterns.

The work will include server updates, database optimization, and security
patches implementation. Our technical team has been planning this for several
months to minimize any potential disruptions.

Thank you for your understanding.
IT Department

<OK>
Subject: Action Required: System Downtime Affects Your Work on March 15

Dear Marketing Team,

**Your CRM system will be unavailable on March 15, 2-4pm.**

**How this affects you:**
- You cannot access client records during this time
- Schedule client calls before 2pm or after 4pm
- Download any reports you need by March 14

**Why we're doing this:**
This upgrade will make your system 50% faster and more secure.

Questions? Reply to this email.

IT Department
```

## Make responding easy
* Simplify the steps required to act
* Organize key information needed for action
* Minimize the amount of attention required

Example:
```
<NG>
Subject: Quick question

Hi Sarah,

Hope you're doing well! I wanted to get your input on something we've been
discussing. We're trying to figure out the best approach for the client
presentation next month. There are a few different options we're considering.

Can you take a look at the attached document when you get a chance and let me
know your thoughts? Also, if you have any suggestions for the timeline, that
would be great. We should probably set up a meeting to discuss this further.

Let me know what works for you!

Thanks,
Mike

<OK>
Subject: Need Your Decision: Client Presentation Format by Friday

Hi Sarah,

**Question:** Should we use slides or a live demo for the TechCorp presentation?

**Context:**
- Slides: safer, easier to control timing
- Live demo: more engaging, higher risk

**What I need:** Reply with "slides" or "demo" by Friday 3pm.

**Next step:** I'll create the materials once you decide.

Thanks,
Mike
```
